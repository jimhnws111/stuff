Filaments, strands or hooks, increasing in coverage and generally thickening as a whole.

Cirrus clouds are generally composed of ice crystals and found between 20,000 and 60,000 feet. Cirrus is usually white, as the cloud have undergone glaciation, a process in which any supercooled water droplets are transformed into ice crystals. A general increasing in coverage of cirrus, as well as thickening, can indicate the approach of a cold front or area of low pressure, portending the eventual arrival of precipitation.

However, thickening cirrus can simply indicate a patch of moisture linked to a mid-level trough. In this case, the clouds generally pass with no adverse weather associated with them.
