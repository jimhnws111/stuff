Toms River Climate Information

Dense cirrus remaining from a CB anvil

Once the upper part of an cumulonimbus (CB) clouds, these cirrus are the dense anvil shaped remains. As the anvil dissipates, the anvil is left behind. Though they can maintain their shape for a time after the dissipation of the CB, these clouds gradually spread out and dissipate.

Across the Northeast, it is uncommon to see this type of cirrus, as trees and other clouds often obscure the sky when thunderstorms are present.
